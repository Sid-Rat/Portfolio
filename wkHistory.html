My Origan Story

For a detailed work history:


I grew up in a restaurant family. So, I unofficially have been working since I could stand.
I officially joined the workforce my senior year of high school. My father was on disability so I got a full time job cooking at the local tourist stop, the Essenhause. I had to clock in before school was out so I worked out an early release agreement with the principal. I cooked in, and managed a couple of restaurants until I got married and started a family.

It was then that I felt like I should have a career, so I went to school for a CDL. I was an over the road truck driver for almost ten years, and yes, I did manage to be home enough to start that family. My daughter was born in '01, and I was home to see her birth. About a year later I got a job hauling equipment for an excavating company so I could be home with my family. Unfortunately all that home time lead to my divorce. After a rough couple of years with alcohol I sobered up and bought my family business.

I took the advise of my accountant and started the umbrella company Maple Leaf Ventures. Under that umbrella I mainly owned and operated the County Seat Cafe. After just shy of ten years I got an offer to sell, and took it.
That lead to my job working the night shift at Polywood. I liked the job, and I liked the night shift, but the factory life got to be too much for me. Through my hobby of gaming and digital character design I discoverd a passion for coding. I was about a quarter of the way through my first game when I discovered I was quite good at coding in Python. That leads me to, hopefully, the last chapter of a long and eventful work experiences.

In early 2021 I started my business SidRat Games and began building websites for local business. After several months of ramon noodles and peanut butter and jelly sandwiches I decided to enroll at Eleven Fifty Academy.I have learned a great deal to support my self taught knowledge of code. Most importantly I have learned coding structure and industry standards. I have a thirst for code in general, and whatever path I choose, I will always be learning new ways to develope software.
